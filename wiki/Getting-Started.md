# Getting Started

## 1. Make a worksheet

**File → New Excel Template** → save an `.xlsx` file.

This is your article list. One row represents one article.

---

## 2. Fill in PubMed info

1. Open the worksheet in Excel.
2. Type PMIDs in the **Ref** column (or select cells that already have them).
3. In Pub-Xel, click **Import PubMed Data**.

Pub-Xel fills in title, journal, abstract, and other columns you turned on in Preferences.

---

## 3. Set your folders

**File → Preferences → Library**

- **Main library** — folder where you keep article PDFs

Click **Save and Exit**, then restart Pub-Xel when asked.

**Name your PDFs PMID.pdf** (e.g., `33301246.pdf`), and save them all in your **Main library**. Don't worry about losing them. As long as you have your Excel Worksheet, you can access the files freely.

---

## 4. Save your PDFs

Download articles from PubMed and save the PDFs in your **Main library** folder. Use the PMID as the filename (e.g., 33301246.pdf).

---

## 5. See what you have

Select PMID cells in Excel → click **Check if Files Exist**.

Cells with a matching PDF turn **blue**.

---

## 6. Open PDFs quickly

1. Copy PMID cells in Excel (**Ctrl+C**).
2. Press **Alt+Shift+K** (or click **Open Files** in Pub-Xel).

Works from Excel, Word, PubMed, and more — see [Clipboard Actions](Clipboard-Actions.md).

---

## 7. Inspect Window

Copy IDs → click **Inspect Files** (or **Alt+Shift+J**).

See which PDFs you have, read PubMed info, open or export files, or make a new worksheet.

---

## Background use

Pub-Xel often stays in the **system tray** after you close the window. Hotkeys still work.

- Tray icon → **Restore** — show the window
- Tray icon → **Exit** — quit completely
